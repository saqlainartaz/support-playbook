# Episode Review & Approval

Use this when a customer is reviewing their episode, approving it, or stuck on the sign-off step.

**Good to know:** There's a **7-day editing window** from when the episode is first delivered.
After that, we move ahead to approval and publishing. Sign-off happens at
**`insidesuccesstv.com/episodeapproval`**.

---

## Situation 1 — Letting them know their episode is ready to approve

Use this to send the review-and-approval request once the episode is ready.

**Reply you can send:**
> Hi [their first name],
> Thank you again for your incredible work on this project. Your episode is now ready for final
> approval.
> We've implemented your edits and ensured this version reflects your vision as closely as
> possible.
> As a reminder, the 7-day editing window from the original delivery has now passed. At this
> stage, we need to move forward with approval and publishing.
> Please review your episode here: 👉 [episode review link]
> Please sign here so we can proceed with publishing:
> insidesuccesstv.com/episodeapproval
> Once approved, we'll finalize and notify you as soon as your episode is live.
> Warm regards,

## Situation 2 — "It won't let me sign off / the approval link isn't working"

**What they usually say:** They love the episode but can't finish the sign-off — the page won't
let them (sometimes with a screenshot).

**What to do:** Reassure them the link works and walk them through the quick fix.

**Reply you can send:**
> Hi [their first name],
> The link is working — you may just need to switch browsers or try a different device:
> insidesuccesstv.com/episodeapproval
> Thanks,
> [Your name]
> Client Concierge Manager

**Watch out for:**
- First fix is always a **different browser or device** (and try switching Wi-Fi and cellular).
- If it's still broken after that, get the web/tech owner on it rather than leaving them stuck.

---

## Situation 3 — "What's the status?" / "Is my episode live yet?"

**What to do:**
1. On Monday.com, open the **Video board → ISTV status**. Always check the current status **and**
   the comment section for the last communication with the team.
2. If the status is **V1 or V2 edit/review**, the team is still working on the episode. Check how
   many days since filming — if it's under ~90 days, it's still within the delivery timeline, so
   the client just needs to wait. Use the *Still in editing* reply.
3. If the status is **"Pending client approval,"** we've already sent them the episode — they need
   to review and submit any edits. **Always resend the episode link.** Use the *Pending approval —
   resend the episode* reply.

**Replies you can send:**

*Still in editing* (status V1/V2, within timeline)
> Hi [their first name],
> Thanks for checking in! Your episode is still with our editing team and on track. Episodes are
> typically delivered within about 90 days of filming, so yours is still within that window —
> we'll let you know the moment it's ready for you to review.
> Best,
> [Your name]
> Client Concierge Manager

*Pending approval — resend the episode*
> Hi [their first name],
> It looks like we sent your episode on [date] and we haven't heard back yet — I'll include the
> email we sent you below:
>
> Hi [their first name],
> Thank you again for your incredible work on this project. Your episode is now ready for final
> approval.
> We've implemented your edits and ensured this version reflects your vision as closely as
> possible.
> As a reminder, the 7-day editing window from the original delivery has now passed. At this
> stage, we need to move forward with approval and publishing.
> Please review your episode here: 👉 [episode review link]
> Please sign here so we can proceed with publishing:
> insidesuccesstv.com/episodeapproval
> Once approved, we'll finalize and notify you as soon as your episode is live.
> Warm regards,
> [Your name]
> Client Concierge Manager

**Watch out for:**
- Press releases and go-live announcements go out as their own separate emails.
- If they want creative changes after the editing window, that's a call for the production /
  editing team — don't promise re-edits yourself.
