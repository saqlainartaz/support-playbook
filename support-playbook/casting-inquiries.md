# Casting Inquiries

Use this when someone has questions about their casting call — how to prepare, timing, or
whether they've been cast.

---

## Situation — Casting prep or confusion about the deadline

**What they usually say:** They're getting ready for their casting call and are confused about
the cut-off date versus their own scheduled call.

**What to do:**
1. If it's a casting decision or date you can't confirm yourself, say so plainly and tell them
   you're checking with the casting team.
2. Give them the one reassurance that's always safe: the date that matters for their review is
   **their own casting call**, not the general cohort date in the email.

**Reply you can send:**
> Hi [their first name],
> I'm not in the casting department, but I've asked the team to confirm the cut-off date for you.
> The date that counts for your review is your own casting call on [date] — the timing is based
> on when your call happens, not the general date mentioned in the email.
> Thank you,
> [Your name]
> Client Concierge Manager

**Watch out for:**
- **Stay in your lane.** Casting decisions, deadlines, and "am I cast?" belong to the casting
  team. Reassure, loop them in, and tell the customer you've done so — don't make the call
  yourself.

## Quick reference — how casting works

Keep this handy so your answers are accurate:

- The casting call **is the audition.** We get 500+ applications a day and pick a limited number.
- Two things decide it: **(1) Alignment** — does their brand and story fit the audience; and
  **(2) the green light** — if they're approved on the call, a quick social-media/Google check
  runs, and the team emails by about **10am EST the next day** to confirm whether they're cast.
- The current group **closes Sunday at 11:59pm.** If accepted, a **second call** covers
  onboarding/scripting and a date to fly to **Miami** within 2026.
- **How to tell them to prepare:** review the info page from when they booked; look at the studio
  mission and current cast; watch at least one episode (the CEO's episode is a great one); and be
  professional on camera — good lighting, not filming from a car.
- **Studio:** 1210 Washington Ave, Unit 250, Miami, FL 33139.
- **More detail:** the *How casting works* article in the Help Center.
