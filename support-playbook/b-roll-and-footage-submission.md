# B-Roll & Footage Submission

Use this when a customer asks how to send in their B-roll or other supporting footage.

---

## Situation — "How and where do I send my footage? What's allowed?"

**What they usually say:** They're gathering footage and want to know how to send it — can they
use a hosting site, is iPhone video okay, can they include an outside clip (like a news
interview)?

**What to do:**
1. Tell them the one accepted way to submit: a Google Drive or Dropbox folder link.
2. Cover the quick rules (horizontal video, short clips, labeled).
3. Point them to the Help Center guides if they want more detail.

**Reply you can send:**
> Hi [their first name],
> Thank you!
> We only accept B-roll via Google Drive or Dropbox — please move everything onto one of those,
> then share the folder link to this support email.
> Interviews can be filmed on an iPhone, but they must be filmed **horizontal**.
> You're welcome to send in the news segment too — congrats!
> Thank you,
> [Your name]
> Client Concierge Manager

**The rules to pass on:**
- **One way to submit:** a **Google Drive or Dropbox folder link**, emailed to the support
  inbox, set to **"Anyone with the link can view."** (They can keep their footage on another
  site like Pixieset, but it has to be moved to Drive or Dropbox to send to us.)
- **iPhone footage is fine** if it's clear and well-lit — and **must be horizontal.**
- **Outside / news footage is okay** if it's relevant.
- Keep clips **short (about 10–60 seconds)**, organized, and labeled. No hours of unlabeled
  footage. For a specific moment in a longer clip, label the file and note the timestamp.

**Watch out for:**
- Helpful articles to share: *B-roll and photo submission guide* and *Pre-shoot checklist and
  updates before your shoot*.
- If they're really asking whether certain footage fits their episode (a creative call), loop in
  the script / production team.
